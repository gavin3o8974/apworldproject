2010

STUXNET

NATION-STATE WEAPON

The world's first known cyberweapon. Created jointly by the US and Israel to sabotage Iran's nuclear program. It physically destroyed uranium centrifuges by making them spin out of control while reporting normal readings. A new era of warfare had begun.

Damage: Set Iran's nuclear program back 2 years

2013

CRYPTOLOCKER

RANSOMWARE PIONEER

The first major ransomware to use Bitcoin for payment. Encrypted victims' files with military-grade encryption and demanded $300–$2,000 to unlock. Infected over 500,000 machines in 3 months. Pioneered a criminal business model that now earns billions per year.

Damage: $30M+ — Ransomware era begins

2016

MIRAI

IOT BOTNET

Targeted IoT devices — cameras, routers, DVRs — with default passwords. Built a botnet of 600,000 devices and launched the largest DDoS attack in history, taking down Dyn DNS and knocking Twitter, Netflix, Reddit, and CNN offline for hours. Written by college students.

Damage: Half the US internet went offline

WANNACRY

RANSOMWARE PANDEMIC

Used a stolen NSA exploit called EternalBlue. Spread to 150 countries in hours. Shut down the UK's NHS hospitals — surgeries were cancelled. Hit FedEx, Telefónica, Russian banks, and Chinese universities. Total damage: $4–8 billion. Attributed to North Korea.

Damage: $4–8B — 200K systems, 150 countries

SUNBURST (SolarWinds)

SUPPLY CHAIN ATTACK

Russian SVR hackers embedded malware into SolarWinds' Orion software update. 18,000 organizations — including the US Treasury, Pentagon, DHS, and Microsoft — installed the update. Attackers had access for months before detection. The most sophisticated hack in history.

Damage: US govt compromised — Billions in response
