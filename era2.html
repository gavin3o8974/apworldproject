1990s

THE WILD WEST

The internet exploded. So did the viruses.

1991

MICHELANGELO

MEDIA PANIC

A boot sector virus set to trigger every March 6th — Michelangelo's birthday. News media predicted millions of computers would be destroyed. The actual damage was far smaller, but it sparked mass public awareness of computer viruses for the first time.

Damage: Moderate — ~20,000 machines
VARIANT INFECTIONS TRACKED28,400
1992

DARK AVENGER

POLYMORPHIC

Bulgarian hacker known as Dark Avenger pioneered polymorphic viruses — code that mutates itself to evade antivirus detection. His Mutation Engine (MtE) was released as a tool kit and gave birth to an entire generation of shape-shifting malware.

Damage: High — Inspired millions of variants
VARIANT INFECTIONS TRACKED1,480,000
1995

CONCEPT

MACRO VIRUS

The first macro virus, spreading through Microsoft Word documents. It proved that viruses didn't need to be executable programs — a Word file was enough. It spread faster than any virus before it, riding the wave of document sharing in offices worldwide.

Damage: Moderate — First macro epidemic
VARIANT INFECTIONS TRACKED2,340,000
1998

CIH (CHERNOBYL)

HARDWARE DESTROYER

Created by Chen Ing-hau, a Taiwanese student. Triggered on April 26th (Chernobyl anniversary). It overwrote the BIOS chip — making computers physically unable to boot. The first virus capable of destroying hardware. Over 1 million PCs destroyed in a single day.

Damage: $1B+ — Bricked 1M machines
VARIANT INFECTIONS TRACKED1,060,000
1999

MELISSA

EMAIL WORM

David L. Smith posted a macro virus disguised as a Word doc on Usenet. When opened, it emailed itself to the first 50 contacts in the victim's Outlook. Email servers at Intel, Microsoft, and the US government crashed. The creator was sentenced to 20 months in prison.

Damage: $80M — Servers worldwide crashed
VARIANT INFECTIONS TRACKED1,230,000
