GLOBAL PANDEMIC

Billions online. Billions at risk.

2000

ILOVEYOU

MOST DESTRUCTIVE

A Visual Basic Script disguised as a love letter. Sent from the Philippines by Onel de Guzman. It overwrote files, stole passwords, and emailed itself to every contact. 10% of all internet-connected computers infected within 10 days. $10 billion in damage.

Damage: $10B — 50M machines infected
VARIANT INFECTIONS TRACKED51,200,000
2001

CODE RED

SERVER WORM

Exploited a buffer overflow in Microsoft IIS. Infected 359,000 machines in 14 hours. It defaced websites with "HACKED BY CHINESE!" and launched DDoS attacks against the White House. The US government had to change the White House's IP address to survive.

Damage: $2B — 359K servers in 14 hours
VARIANT INFECTIONS TRACKED412,000
2003

SQL SLAMMER

FASTEST EVER

A 376-byte worm that exploited Microsoft SQL Server. It doubled in size every 8.5 seconds. It infected 75,000 hosts in 10 minutes and caused massive internet slowdowns worldwide — including taking South Korea offline. The fastest-spreading worm in history.

Damage: $1B+ — Internet slowdown worldwide
VARIANT INFECTIONS TRACKED87,000
2004

MyDoom

FASTEST EMAIL WORM

Still holds the record for the fastest-spreading email worm ever. At its peak, 1 in 12 emails globally was a MyDoom infection. It launched DDoS attacks on SCO Group and Microsoft. Origins remain unknown — believed to be commissioned by Russian spam groups.

Damage: $38B — 1 in 12 emails infected
VARIANT INFECTIONS TRACKED52,700,000
2008

CONFICKER

BOTNET KING

Exploited a Windows vulnerability to build a botnet of 9–15 million machines. It was so sophisticated it had a peer-to-peer update mechanism and couldn't be killed. Infected military computers, hospitals, and governments. Still active today on some unpatched systems.

Damage: $9B — 15M machine botnet
